In English grammar, a subject is the noun, pronoun, or phrase that performs the action or controls the verb in a sentence. For example, in the sentence "The dog chased the ball," the subject is "The dog". It typically appears at the beginning of a sentence.

Geography is the study of the Earth's physical features, human societies, and the dynamic interactions between the two. It seeks to understand where things are, why they are located there, and how they develop and change over space and time.

Mathematics (often abbreviated as "math" or "maths") is the abstract study of numbers, quantities, space, and structures. It serves as the foundational language for science, technology, engineering, and finance, allowing us to model the world and solve complex, real-world problems.
